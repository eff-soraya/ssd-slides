"Passphrases, what are those?"
Similar to the example in the comic, passphrases are:

• generally 4+ words

• unique

• long

• random

• hard for computers and people to guess

• easy for you to remember

"But how do I remember all these different passphrases?"
Password Manager!

• Lots of options available, e.g. KeePass, 1Password, LastPass.

• Encrypts your passwords.

• Just requires one passphrase (master passphrase) to remember, which unlocks the others.

• Great for saving regular passwords and security questions.

Protecting your data
Saving to the cloud

• If you upload your data to a cloud service—Google Drive, Dropbox, iCloud—that data can potentially be read by the service provider.

• Law enforcement can send a subpoena to the provider for the data.

• Depending on your threat model, you may not want to upload to cloud services.

Securely deleting your data on your devices

• When you delete a file from your computer, you’re only deleting it from the “table of contents” of your drive.

• Data still there until something else needs to use those unused “pages.”

• A “secure” delete writes random data into the place where the file was.

For Mac OSX
• On OS X 10.4 to 10.10, you can securely delete files by moving them to the Trash and then selecting Finder > Secure Empty Trash.
• The Secure Empty Trash feature was removed in OS X 10.11, because Apple felt that it could not guarantee secure deletion on the fast flash—Solid State Drives—that most of its modern models now use. The same is true for flash drives.
For Windows
https://www.bleachbit.org/download/windows
Again, secure deletion on the fast flash—Solid State Drives (SSDs)—and USB drives is not guaranteed.
Full Disk Encryption:

encrypts every single file on your devices

Since we may all be using SSDs, a crucial thing you should do is encrypt the data on your devices.
Why use full disk encryption?

• Protects all data on your computer, when your computer is turned off.

• Use case: Protects if computer is seized or stolen

Encrypting your disk on Mac OSX

• Use FireVault!

On Macs, you can turn on FileVault by going into your Security & Privacy settings
Windows

• Can use BitLocker but *only* available on the Pro and Enterprise versions of Windows

And don't forget to encrypt your phones!

• Protects all data on your phone, when the screen is locked.

• Use case: Protects if phone is seized or stolen.

• Have a good password (NOT biometric fingerprint) to protect your phone!

Android

• offered under its "Security" settings

iOS (iPhones, iPads, etc.)

• encryption turned on if you set a passcode.

If you're concerned about anonymity, Tor is great because:

• Clears search history each session.

• Goes through a different IP address that is hard to route back to the initial user.

• Removes browser identifiability (if you use it correctly).

• Blocks javascript by default.

• Has HTTPS Everywhere enabled.

• Run by a nonprofit and dedicated volunteers.
