What are some of the things you should keep in mind when making a password?

You should make passphrases!
"Passphrases, what are those?"
Similar to the example in the comic, passphrases are:

+ generally 4+ words

+ unique

+ long

+ hard for computers to guess.

+ It's best if they are random words.

"But how do I remember all these different passphrases?"
Password Managers!
Password managers

+ Lots of options available, e.g. KeePass, 1Password, LastPass.

+ Encrypts your passwords.

+ Just requires one password (master password) to remember, which unlocks the others.

+ Great for saving passwords and security questions.

"So using HTTPS encrypts the data I submit and makes it safer for me to browse the web...but what about the metadata around that? Can't advertisers (and others) use 'cookies' to track me when I visit a site?"

Browser tracking can be a huge problem. Lots of little pieces of information submitted over time can make a pretty comprehensive profile of who you are, and what you might be looking at. Privacy Badger stops part of this problem by stopping tracking cookies.
However...

Online trackers and sites themselves may be able to identify you – even if you’ve installed software to protect yourself.

It’s possible to configure your browser to thwart tracking, but many people don’t know how.

Questions about browsers, HTTP/HTTPS, tracking?

If you're concerned about anonymity, Tor is great because:

-Clears search history each session.

-Goes through a different IP address that is hard to route back to the initial user.

-Removes browser identifiability (if you use it corectly)

-Blocks javascript by default.

-Has HTTPS Everywhere enabled.

-Run by a nonprofit and dedicated volunteers.

Encrypting your devices
Good basic security:

Screen lock your phone.

Password protect your computer.

Put your devices "to sleep" when leaving them. Do not leave them unlocked.

Shut down a device completely when traveling.

Password protecting your phone, computer, and other devices
So you've picked an alphanumeric code. What kind of password should you make?
You should make a PASSPHRASE!(4+ words)
"Ok, for my computer password and phone password, that makes sense. But what about all my other passwords?"
